The same problems keep showing up - just wearing different logos.

SaaS Platforms

"We're losing trials to a competitor with a worse product - just a better UI."

Users decide in seconds. If the first 30 seconds of your trial feel confusing, they're gone - and they're not converting to paid.

"Our expansion revenue is flat. Power users love it - everyone else barely uses it."

Shallow activation means shallow expansion. If your UX buries the features that drive upgrade decisions, you're leaving NRR on the table every month.

"We quoted a $40K redesign to our board. They said come back with something realistic."

You have a roadmap, a burn rate, and a Series B to close. You need a redesign that ships on a timeline that doesn't require a board vote.

AI SaaS Products

"Users don't understand what our AI actually does. They sign up, poke around, and churn."

AI products fail at UX when the output is powerful but the interface doesn't explain the value. Confusion kills retention before the product gets a chance.

"Enterprise buyers are nervous about the 'black box' in demos. We're losing deals."

At $50K+ ACV, buyers need to see control, not magic. When your AI interface doesn't communicate confidence and transparency, trust erodes before the contract hits legal.

"Our last designer had never seen a model inference call. The handoff was a disaster."

Designing AI products requires understanding latency states, confidence scores, fallback UX, and streaming outputs. Generic designers don't know what they don't know.

Fintech / Payments

"Our onboarding takes 9 steps. We know it. Users know it. Our NPS knows it."

In fintech, friction is a conversion killer. Every extra step is users abandoning your funnel for a competitor.

"We're regulated. Lawyers approved the copy. Nobody approved how unreadable it is."

Compliance copy doesn't have to mean broken UX. We've redesigned regulated products — disclosures, KYC steps, consent flows — without touching what legal approved.

"We launched a new product and it looks nothing like our main platform. Users are confused."

Fragmented design systems drive up support tickets and signal immaturity to enterprise buyers. A unified design language fixes that.

How do you handle AI-specific UX patterns - like streaming outputs, confidence scores, or model latency? +
We've designed for them. Streaming text, loading states for inference calls, fallback UI when a model returns low confidence, human-in-the-loop review flows - these are patterns we've built and iterated on across multiple AI product clients. Your designer won't be learning on the job.
We're in a regulated Fintech space. Can you design around compliance constraints without breaking the UX? +
Yes. We've worked with KYC flows, AML disclosures, investment disclaimers, and payment consent patterns. We redesign around your legal requirements - not through them. Your lawyers keep what they approved. Your users get a flow that doesn't make them want to close the tab.
We have an internal design team. Why would we bring you in? +
Most of our SaaS and Fintech clients do. We come in to ship a specific redesign - a core activation flow, an onboarding overhaul, a dashboard rebuild - while your internal team handles feature work. No politics, no ramp-up time, fast output.
Is quality actually the same with AI in the workflow? +
Better, in most cases. AI removes the work that doesn't require a senior designer - research synthesis, first-pass wireframes, regression testing. Your senior designer now spends 100% of their time on what actually moves metrics: product logic, UX hierarchy, and conversion decisions.
